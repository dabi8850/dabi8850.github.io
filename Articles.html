The Best Health And Fitness Apps of 2017

Sworkit—Comprehensive

Sworkit’s extensive video library includes all kinds of workouts, including cardio, yoga, and strength training. You can also sync your results directly to other fitness apps you use, like Apple Health and Google Fit. If you sign up for the premium app, you can even ask questions to a real trainer if you feel like your current regimen just isn’t cutting it. (Free: iOS / Android)

JEFIT—Comprehensive

If you’re focusing on bodybuilding, then JEFIT is what you want. Their database includes over 1,300 exercises, and allows you to input your own weight and reps for accurate results. The JEFIT landscape includes thousands of community-created plans for every skill level, whether you’ve never used a barbell or you’re the next Schwarzenegger. (Free: iOS / Android)

Johnson & Johnson Official Seven-Minute Workout—Quick-hit

You’ve probably heard of the “seven-minute workout” at this point—it’s become a favorite among fitness fans who don’t have much time to spare. And while there are plenty of apps that follow this workout methodology, the Johnson & Johnson app is the only one made with support from Chris Jordan, who helped design the original workout four years ago. The app includes a number of pre-designed workouts as well as the option to create your own, and offers video of each exercise being performed so you know exactly what you’re supposed to do. (Free: iOS / Android).

Seven—Quick-hit

An alternative to the official Seven-Minute Workout app, Seven puts its own spin on the quick-hit original. This includes a “7-Month Challenge” to encourage you to stay consistent, a tracker that shows your progress day-by-day, and even video game-style achievements. Plus, you can customize which workouts you want based on your specific needs, whether you want to lose weight or you just want to push yourself to your limits. (Free: iOS / Android)

Keelo—Quick-hit

Keelo’s specialty is High-Intensity Interval Training. The app includes a massive video library walking you through each exercise step by step, with or without equipment. You can see exactly how long each exercise is and how many calories it burns, and you can even mark certain exercises as favorites so you can go back to them later. If the amount of exercises is overwhelming, you can always follow one of the pre-established workout plans and start burning that fat immediately. (Free: iOS / Android)

The Key To A Good Lifting Session Lies Within Your Playlist

According to Costas Karageorghis, a sports psychologist and the deputy head of research for the School of Sport and Education at Brunel University in London, a simple formula can tell you whether or not your playlist is making you work harder. After 20 years of research and years building playlists for top athletes, Karageorghis found that BPM (beats per minute) is one of the main factors at making you work harder. Karageorghis also found through his research and experience that the most effective songs are those that distract your brain—the ones that allow you to zone out and ignore what's happening to your body as you workout. The more a song takes your focus off of the pain of your workout, the less effort you'll expend and the easier it'll feel.
